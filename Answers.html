C1 Advanced – Reading Answer Key (with Explanations)

Teacher Copy – Modern English Australia · Koala Spies Sydney

Part 1 – Studying Platypuses

  1. 1. A – insight
    Explanation: “The insight this gave her…” fits grammatically and semantically — it means deep understanding. The other options don’t convey the same meaning.
  2. 2. C – Contrary
    Explanation: The phrase “Contrary to popular belief” introduces contrast with what most people assume — this is a fixed expression.
  3. 3. C – avoid
    Explanation: “Avoid human contact” makes logical sense — it means to stay away from. Other verbs don’t fit collocationally.
  4. 4. B – denies
    Explanation: “She denies the long-held claim” means she rejects it as untrue. It’s the correct collocation for a “claim.”
  5. 5. A – illusions
    Explanation: The fixed expression “under no illusions” means to have a realistic view. “Illusions” is the only noun that fits naturally.
  6. 6. C – extend
    Explanation: “Does not extend beyond…” means it doesn’t go further than — grammatically and logically correct.
  7. 7. B – provided
    Explanation: “Has provided valuable opportunities” is the standard collocation — means to offer or supply something useful.
  8. 8. A – taught
    Explanation: “The project has taught her…” fits idiomatically and links to what she has learned through experience.

Part 2 – The True Cost of Style

  1. 0. C – Analytical but cautiously optimistic
    Explanation: The writer’s tone is thoughtful, acknowledging challenges but suggesting positive change is possible.
  2. 1. C – Sustainable fashion was initially underestimated but is now gaining importance
    Explanation: The paragraph contrasts early dismissal with its new significance, matching this summary.
  3. 2. B – The environmental and social damage outweighs the low prices
    Explanation: “The real cost is paid elsewhere” implies hidden harm — not financial but ecological and ethical.
  4. 3. A – Reducing waste through creative reuse
    Explanation: “Slow fashion” promotes using existing materials and upcycling — exactly this idea.
  5. 4. B – Consumers must take responsibility for their buying habits
    Explanation: The insider’s quote emphasizes personal accountability, not just brand promises.
  6. 5. A – It is declining as people become more aware of its impact
    Explanation: The paragraph shows fast fashion’s appeal fading due to awareness of exploitation and waste.
  7. 6. A – They are making a genuine effort to change the industry
    Explanation: Designers using new materials and methods are portrayed positively as innovators.
  8. 7. B – Consumers and companies share responsibility for sustainable change
    Explanation: The final paragraph links both groups together in the need for conscious action.
  9. 8. A – People must value creativity over consumption
    Explanation: “Redefine what success and self-expression look like” suggests prioritizing meaning and creativity, not buying.

Part 3 – The Psychology of Decision-Making

  1. 0. B – In such moments, intuition often takes over...
    Explanation: Example sentence provided in the question illustrating instinctive decision-making.
  2. 1. C – These habits once helped our ancestors act quickly under pressure, but today they often lead to snap judgments and costly mistakes.
    Explanation: Fits perfectly with “heuristics” — explains their origin and modern drawbacks.
  3. 2. D – Advertisers exploit this by appealing to our emotions rather than our logic, knowing that feelings often override facts.
    Explanation: Expands on emotion’s influence on decision-making and gives a real-world example.
  4. 3. A – As our energy drops, we often default to easier, less thoughtful options — from unhealthy meals to unnecessary online purchases.
    Explanation: Directly relates to “decision fatigue” — loss of willpower and impulsive behaviour.
  5. 4. E – Paradoxically, this abundance of choice can leave us feeling anxious and indecisive instead of free and empowered.
    Explanation: Concludes logically about the negative psychological effect of too much choice.
